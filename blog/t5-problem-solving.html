Problem Solving

A time when I got blocked on a simple problem.

I thought I was doing a good thing when I downloaded 'Eloquent JS' as a pdf. I had the entire book rather than just the online chapter that was linked to in the instructions. When it came to the tasks at the end of the chapter however, I got really stuck because the pdf didn't have the same interactive content as the online chapter. The online chapter had a space to paste and check your code, whereas the pdf didn't. I became quite confused and overcompicated the problem trying to work out ways to test my code. Techniques I used were to try something, Googling and asking my peers for help. Of course, the final technique produced an immediate result. On reflection, this was something very straightforward that I didn't even consider when problem solving myself. Re-thinking the task from the very beginning would have been beneficial.

A time when I solved a problem in an eloquent way.

I was working on one of the more complicated JavaScript exercises and the logic was overwhelming me. I had tried posting on slack and received some good tips, but I still couldn't resolve the problem. I decided to pseudocode the logic, and found that this was a great technique for this type of problem. The code itself was confusing me, but writing it out in step by step sentences helped to give a lot more clarity. Having gone through this process, and then revisited the tips on Slack, I was able to resolve the problem and I also learned how to better understand the code.
